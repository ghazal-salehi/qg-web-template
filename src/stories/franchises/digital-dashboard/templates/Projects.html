DJAG Blue Card NRS Integration On track $2.4 million 12-10-2019 16-12-2022
  • Objectives:

    The NRS Integration project was initiated in response to the Royal Commission into Institutional Responses to Child Sexual Abuse (Royal Commission) report (released in 2015) on Working with Children Checks (WWCC), highlighting a key issue with the lack of visibility of WWCC outcomes across jurisdictions.

    In the context of this issue, the Royal Commission recommended the implementation of a centralised information repository to share key WWCC decisions about individuals who have been prohibited from working with children across all jurisdictions which enables WWCC operators to make more informed assessments. In July 2019, the Federal Department of Human Services (DHS) and the Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission (ACIC) delivered the National Reference System (NRS) which is able to record, maintain and broadcast key decisions, by all jurisdictions for WWCCs and assist in the continuous monitoring of key decisions where a person is subsequently prohibited from working with children. Each jurisdiction is responsible for integrating their own systems with the NRS.

    The purpose of this project is to enable Queensland's blue card system to participate in this exchange. This requires both system and legislative change.

  • Expenditure:
    • Cost to date: $2.4 million
    • Approved expenditure: $2.4 million
    • Project commencement allocation: $1.1 million
  • Timeframe:
    • Start date: 12-10-2019
    • Approved end date: 16-12-2022
  • Project journey & reasons for variance:

    • Scope Change: Cost Re-evaluation: Delivery Delay:

    Stage 1 - Discovery and Analysis phase commenced in 2019 and detailed business requirements and a high level conceptual technical design were completed. It was originally anticipated that the Stage 2 - Delivery phase would be completed in 2020, however, the project was placed temporarily on hold due to: (1) the need to prioritise the implementation of the IT system changes required to support the Blue Card System's No Card, No Start laws on 31 August 2020; and (2) the impacts of the COVID-19 health emergency on QLD Government initiatives through the Savings and Debt Plan.

    The project was formally re-initiated in July 2021 and funded through an approved budget deferral. When the project was re-initiated, more detailed planning was completed and the project schedule and delivery approach was revised. During this process, it was identified that additional budget would be required to complete the project with the production release scheduled for the first half of 2022.

    The project status changed to 'Action Required' in April 2022 given a number of impacts (for example challenges in retaining and recruiting specialist contractors) effecting the planned velocity of project delivery. Since this time, recruitment and onboarding of the required specialist resources has been completed. A re-evaluation of the required delivery time and cost was undertaken and approved through an exception reporting process and the project returned to 'On Track' status effective end of June 2022.

    The project has now completed development,testing and other assurance activities and was successfully deployed to production on 14 December 2022 in parallel with legislation coming into effect. Project closure activities are now underway including development, testing and deployment of a warranty release and transitioning support for the technical solutions and business processes to operational teams. Going forward it will not be reported on the Digital Dashboard.
